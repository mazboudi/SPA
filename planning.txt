Operating Princiles
Pillar 1: Reliable & Secure Digital Workplace
Purpose: Ensure employees can work without disruption, while protecting the enterprise.
What this pillar represents
•	High availability of devices, tools, and collaboration platforms
•	Security embedded into the end-user experience (not bolted on)
•	Proactive risk reduction vs. reactive incident response
Typical data used to justify this pillar
•	Device uptime and failure rates
•	Incident volumes, severity, MTTR
•	Security posture metrics (patch compliance, vulnerability remediation)
Outcome statement
“Employees trust their digital workplace to be stable, secure, and always available.”

Pillar 2: Frictionless Employee Experience
Purpose: Minimize effort for employees to get work done.
What this pillar represents
•	Simple, intuitive tools and processes
•	Reduced handoffs, tickets, and wait times
•	Experience measured from the employee’s perspective, not IT’s
Typical data used
•	Ticket trends by category
•	First-contact resolution
•	Employee satisfaction / experience scores
•	Self-service adoption
Outcome statement
“Technology enables productivity rather than becoming a barrier.”
________________________________________
Pillar 3: Operational Excellence & Scale
Purpose: Deliver consistent service efficiently across locations, roles, and growth.
What this pillar represents
•	Standardization where it matters
•	Clear operating models and ownership
•	Automation and repeatability
Typical data used
•	Cost per user / per device
•	Volume growth vs. headcount growth
•	Automation rates
•	SLA performance
Outcome statement
“We scale services without scaling complexity or cost.”
________________________________________
Pillar 4: Data Driven Decision Making
Purpose: Run EUC as a measurable, continuously improving operation.
What this pillar represents
•	Decisions grounded in usage, performance, and outcome data
•	Clear KPIs aligned to business impact
•	Transparency for leadership and partners
Typical data used
•	Trend analysis (incidents, requests, performance)
•	Adoption metrics (collaboration tools, features)
•	Benchmarking vs. targets
Outcome statement
“We invest, prioritize, and course correct based on evidence, not anecdotes.”
________________________________________
Pillar 5: Enablement & Change Adoption
Purpose: Ensure technology changes translate into real business value.
What this pillar represents
•	Intentional rollout and adoption strategies
•	Training aligned to roles and workflows
•	Strong partnership with the business
Typical data used
•	Adoption curves post launch
•	Training participation and effectiveness
•	Feature utilization vs. availability
Outcome statement
“New capabilities are adopted quickly and used effectively.”
________________________________________
Pillar 6: Trusted Partnerships & Governance
Purpose: Act as a strategic partner, not just a service provider.
What this pillar represents
•	Clear governance and decision rights
•	Strong vendor and internal partnerships
•	Alignment with enterprise priorities
Typical data used
•	Stakeholder feedback
•	Vendor performance metrics
•	Demand vs. capacity trends
Outcome statement
“EUC is a trusted advisor aligned to enterprise outcomes.”
